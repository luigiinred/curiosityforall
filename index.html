SIMI

Summer Institute on Medical Ignorance

The Summer Institute of Medical Ignorance is a seven-week in-residence experience for disadvantaged high school students. The experience provides each student with the opportunity to work in research-based laboratories and attend lectures on biomedical research. Students produce a capstone project where they put research translation into practice by presenting before an audience of peers and faculty. They also produce, in teams, translational videos, putting into images and words what they have learned to share.

Workshops/Conferences

Held throughout the US and internationally, these educational offerings focus on medical and other ignorance - unanswered questions and unquestioned answers - and provide inquiry tools at multiple educational levels to explore "what we know we don't know don't know we don't know, and think we know but don't" about a wide variety of topics.

T³

Technology, Team and Translation

A variety of technologies are used in CMI to promote blended learning and reach diverse learners in a variety of venues. Learning teams from n=l (individual innovation) to multinational learning communities are encouraged, focusing on the generation and sharing of iterative questions with intermediate answers. Practical application as well as fundamental discovery is encompassed.

K-20 Collaborations

Multilevel partnerships with K-20 higher and professional education and research institutions

Learning/inquiry partnerships are forged and nurtured with bidirectional exchange emphasizing particularly K-12 community and biomedical research/health professional careers with special attention to disadvantaged students and populations and dissemination strategies.

Questionarium Project

This faculty to faculty to student exchange model aims to promote sharing of key basic, clinical and societal questions underlying active or planned research projects with the end result of establishing new collaborations and teams. It is a type of match.com or eharmony for initiating research partnerships and gathering research teams. La Residencia del Incognito Based on a model at the University of Madrid developed by Nobel laureate Severo Ochoa, this residential retreat in Tucson, Arizona "devoted to all we have yet to learn and discover” provides a protected stress-free paperless environment for learners of all ages and experience to engage in reflection, discussion, and interchange. It is home to the Ignorance Foundation.

Pipeline

Stepwise involvement in workforce and career development focuses on facilitating entry and retention in the biomedical workforce and providing engaging inquiry-based experiences in science education and practice.

IgU

Ignorance University

IgU is a new University for the next century where the focus has been shifted from all we know (knowledge factory) to all the things that we have yet to learn and discover. Questions, the questioning process, and questioners (Q³) are the elements of IgU, which also links failure and chaos to the new paradigm.
